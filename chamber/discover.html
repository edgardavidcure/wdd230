Discover Montclair

A city where you will feel welcomed and part of the community

A Place To Live

Montclair's population is about 40.000(est.), with multiple housing options including apartment complexes, mobile homes, one and two story houses. Based on last census, there are at least 9,911 housing units in Montclair

A Shopping Area

With around 813 employer firms (Reference year 2017), Montclair includes a wide variety of businesses, from electronic stores, clothing, shopping centers, and others.

Diversity in Montclair

There are 5 ethnicity groups that are predominant in Montclair: Other (Hispanic) (35.8%), White (Hispanic) (31.8%), White (Non-Hispanic) (13.5%), Asian (Non-Hispanic) (10.5%), and Black or African American (Non-Hispanic) (2.88%). The Montclair Place is a mall that hosts multiple stores and restaurants, with a variety of food choices, going from Mexican food, Asian food, to fast food chain restaurants.

Transportation

The city of Montclair is in the path of the Metrolink system that connects to Los Angeles, with a local train station and Omnitrans facilities to help locals commute. The city also connects with the 10 freeway, which allow locals to have multiple commuting options.
